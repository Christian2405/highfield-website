Frequently Asked Questions

The Occupation Right Agreement is the most common form of occupation and ownership in retirement villages in New Zealand. It gives you the right to occupy your chosen villa for your lifetime, and the right to enjoy the land, buildings and facilities intended for common use in the village.

All villages are governed by the Retirement Villages Act 2003. Under this Act, you are required to get independent legal advice before signing an occupation right agreement.

Under the Retirement Villages Act there is a 15 working day cooling off period after you have signed the Occupation Right Agreement with your Lawyer. During this period you can cancel your agreement without any deductions, and receive your full deposit back with interest.

Costs include the purchase price of your villa, the Village Outgoings fee (a weekly payment of $195 covering the running of the village), and the Village Contribution. You are also responsible for internal maintenance of your villa and personal expenses such as food, power, phone bills, and contents insurance.

The Village Contribution covers the cost of providing village facilities, on-going development, and maintenance costs not covered by the Village Outgoings fee. At Highfield Country Estate, it accrues at a rate of 10% per year up to a maximum of 3 years (equating to 30% of your initial purchase price). It is not charged until termination of the Occupation Right Agreement and your villa is re-sold.

Highfield Country Estate management requires that you must be over 65 years of age, and at the time you purchase an Occupation Right Agreement you must be in good health.
