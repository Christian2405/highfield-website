Our Location

Te Awamutu

The rose town of New Zealand

Nau mai, Haere mai

Literally translated, Te Awamutu means ‘the river cut short’, marking the end of the navigable section of the Manga-o-hoi Stream. Known as the ‘rose town’ for its many elaborate rose gardens, Te Awamutu is a vibrant community with small-town charm.

The Rose Town

Te Awamutu Rose Gardens

Te Awamutu’s spectacular Rose Gardens have earned the town the title of “Rosetown” of New Zealand. Situated on Gorst Avenue, the gardens attract thousands of visitors every year with over 2,500 rose bushes across more than 50 varieties.

The best time to visit while the flowers are in bloom is from November to May. The gardens are a short drive from Highfield Country Estate and a favourite destination for our residents.

Perfectly Connected

Location & Accessibility

Te Awamutu is ideally located just 30 minutes south of Hamilton, the Waikato’s largest city. Auckland is approximately 2 hours north via the Waikato Expressway, making it easy for family visits.

The town is well-served with excellent healthcare facilities, medical centres, pharmacies, and specialist providers. Hamilton’s Waikato Hospital — one of New Zealand’s largest — is just a short drive away.

Life in Te Awamutu

Top Things to Do

Te Awamutu Museum

Te Whare Taonga o Te Awamutu Museum houses a nationally significant collection celebrating local history, social development, and bi-cultural heritage. Free entry with creative activities for all the family.

Alphra Lavender Farm

Just 8 km south of Te Awamutu, Alphra Lavender is a stunning destination open from December to February. Walk through the lavender fields and browse handmade products in the gift shop.

Walk of Fame

Te Awamutu is the hometown of Tim and Neil Finn of Split Enz and Crowded House. The Walk of Fame celebrates the town’s rich musical heritage right in the heart of town.

Lake Ngāroto

Just 8 km from Te Awamutu, Lake Ngāroto is a beautiful peat lake surrounded by native bush. A peaceful spot for walking, birdwatching, and enjoying the natural beauty of the Waikato.

Mount Kakepuku

A prominent volcanic peak near Te Awamutu offering scenic walks through native bush. The mountain is a local landmark and a wonderful day-trip destination for those who enjoy the outdoors.

St John’s Church

One of New Zealand’s most beautiful heritage churches, Historic St John’s Anglican Church is a treasured landmark in Te Awamutu Central, rich with history and architectural beauty.

Natural Beauty

Walks & Outdoors

Te Awamutu is surrounded by lush Waikato farmland with the Pirongia ranges as a scenic backdrop. The Pioneer Walk on Selwyn Lane is a favourite for a gentle stroll through established gardens and heritage features.

Nearby Pirongia Forest Park, Yarndley’s Bush, and the Waitomo Caves offer wonderful day-trip destinations. From championship golf courses to cycling trails and bowling clubs, there is no shortage of ways to stay active.
